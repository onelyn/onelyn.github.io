Stages of brain development

general stages characteristic of brain development in all mammals:

cell birth -> cell migration -> cell differentiation -> cell maturation (dendrite树突 and axon轴突 growth) -> synaptogenesis突触形成 -> cell death and synaptic pruning -> myelogenesis

Fertilization -> three weeks after: cells (to produce the nervous system) begin to form (form the neural tube, later called the subventricular室下 zone -> six weeks of age: cells that form the cerebrum大脑 begin division -> 14 weeks: the cerebrum looks distinctly human -> 5 months: most neurogenesis is complete expect being cells in the hippo-campus (which continues to form neurons throughout life) -> 7 months: sulci沟 and gyri回 begin to form.

Cerebral cortex in each hemisphere: ten billion cells

250,000 neurons per minute

Once the neurons are formed, they begin to migrate along fibrous pathways formed by radial放射状的 glial胶质 cells & extend from the subventricular zone to the surface of the cerebral cortex. The cells formed in a particular subventricular region migrate to a certain cortical location. As the cells reached destination they will be steered to differentiating into a particular cell type.

The formation of dendrites begins prenatally in humans but continues long after birth (slow). Two year after: spines脊柱 (location of most excitatory synapses) are formed. **Axons grow faster **-> reach another cell the dendrites of that cell are completely formed -> influence dendritic differentiation and the formation of cerebral circuits.

Synapse formation is too large -> only the general outlines of neural connections will be genetically predetermined & the vast array of synapses is guided by a variety of environmental cues and signals

Uncertainty in the number of neurons that will reach appropriately -> the brain overproduces -> cell death and synaptic pruning to remove the unwanted (forms like epigenetic signals, experiences, gonadal hormone性腺激素, stress etc) -> the cortex become thinner in a caudal尾-rostral头 gradient梯度 The thinner is better rule.

**Experiences are essentially acting on very different brains at different stages of development. **

Special features of brain development

Two especially important features:

  • The cells lining the subventricular zone are stem根源 cells that remain active throughout life. They stay quiescent静止的 for extended periods but can be activated to produce neurons/glia. Their generation can be influenced by many factors including experience, drugs, hormones and injury.
  • Dendrites and spines show remarkable plasticity in response to experience (overproduction and pruning). There are differences between early brain and later brain in synapses' formation. Early "expecting" experiences and are diffusely扩散 throughout the cerebrum. Later ones are more focal焦点的 and localized to regions involved in processing specific experiences (experiences lead to selective synapse formation and loss -> change neural networks by adding and pruning synapses -> brain plasticity) . experience-expectant vs experience-dependent.

General Principles of Plasticity in Normal Brain

key principles of plasticity:

  • Changes in the brain can be shown at many levels of analysis (global, molecular分子, calcium channels cell morphology形态学 etc)
  • Different measures of neuronal morphology change independently of each other and sometimes in opposite directions. Thus changes in spine density密度 ≠ changes in dendritic length.
  • Experience-dependent changes tend to be focal. A failure to find synaptic changes that correlate with behavioral change is note evidence of the absence of changes. Notice: experience's global nature.
  • Plastic changes are time-dependent. They are not permanent.
  • Experience-dependent changes interact. Later experiences have a much-attenuated effect.
  • Plastic changes are age-dependent. The developing brain will be more responsive to experiences. There are qualitatively different changes in the brain in response to the same experience at different ages.
  • Not all plasticity is good. Plastic changes can interfere with behavior too.

Factors Influencing Brain Development

Even fairly innocuous-looking experiences can profoundly affect brain development.

Sensory and motor experiences

By raising animals in deprived environments and compare with those in standard environments.

Visual:

By placing animals in complex environments in which there is an opportunity for animals to interact with a changing sensory and social environment and to engage in far more motor activity than regular caging.

  4. Early experiences have a powerful effect on brain organization both during development an in adulthood.

Conclusions

Experiences are not singular events but rather as we go through life, experiences interact to alter both behavior and brain, a process often referred to as metaplasticity.

Use "the developing brain" like a single time but still unknown the time in which the developing brain is more/less responsive than at other times.

Different cerebral regions will response at different times. (eg. motor cortex injured in early adolescence -> poor outcome at late adolescence)

Plastic changes in brain organization can be studied at many other levels besides the synaptic plasticity. Ultimately the fundamental mechanism of synaptic change will be found in gene expression. The difficulty is that it is likely that experiences that alter behaviors significantly will be related to changes in dozens or hundreds of genes. The challenge is to identify the changes that are most closely associated with the observed behavioral changes.
